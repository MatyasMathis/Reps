## What Is Reps?

**Reps** is a premium iOS habit and task tracking app designed around the idea that consistency is performance. The name "Reps" is a deliberate nod to athletic training — every task you complete is a "rep," and your streak is your performance score. The app's design is directly inspired by Whoop (the elite fitness wearable), with a dark-mode-first aesthetic: deep black backgrounds, bold recovery-green accents, and clean SF Pro typography.

---

## Core Concept

Reps is not a traditional to-do list app. It reframes daily tasks as a personal performance metric. The core loop is: set up recurring tasks (daily, weekly, monthly, or one-time), check them off each day, watch your streak grow, and review your performance through rich analytics. Completed tasks disappear from your list — leaving only what still needs to be done — and are permanently logged in your history.

---

## Who It's For

- **Habit builders** pursuing daily consistency (workout every day, read 30 minutes, meditate, journal, etc.)
- **High-performers and productivity-obsessed individuals** who treat their life like an athlete treats training
- **People frustrated with bloated, subscription-based task managers** who want something clean, fast, and private
- **iOS users** who love beautiful, native design — widgets, iCloud sync, dark mode

---

## Key Features

- **Today View** — A daily dashboard showing all tasks due today with a live progress card (e.g., "4 of 8 completed — 50%"). Tasks check off with haptic feedback and disappear when done.
- **Recurring Tasks** — Tasks can recur daily, on specific weekdays (e.g., "Gym — Mon/Wed/Fri"), on specific dates each month, or be one-time only.
- **History View** — Browse every completed task by date with a calendar navigator — a visual record of everything you've accomplished.
- **Statistics** *(Pro)* — Deep analytics filtered by category: total reps, current streak, best streak, completion rate, weekly rhythm bar charts, monthly trends, per-task breakdowns, and a calendar heatmap.
- **Year in Pixels** *(Pro)* — A full-year heatmap showing every day's completion status — a visual summary of your entire year of consistency.
- **Sharing** *(Pro)* — Export stats, heatmaps, and year views as images to share on social media.
- **Custom Categories** *(Pro)* — Organize tasks into color-coded, icon-tagged categories (e.g., Health, Work, Learning, Finance).
- **iCloud Sync** *(Pro)* — Tasks and history sync seamlessly across all Apple devices.
- **Home Screen Widget** — A "Today's Tasks" widget in small, medium, and large sizes. See your tasks and progress without opening the app.
- **Daily Notifications** — Optional daily reminder at a user-set time.

## Business Model

- **Free tier**: Core task creation and daily tracking
- **Reps Pro**: $4.99 one-time lifetime purchase (no subscription). Unlocks statistics, Year in Pixels, sharing, custom categories, and iCloud sync.
- **No ads, no data collection, no third-party tracking** — fully privacy-first
- Built natively for Apple ecosystem (iOS 17+)

---

## Design Philosophy

The aesthetic is athletic and minimal — inspired by Whoop's dark performance UI. The visual language communicates *momentum* and *consistency*, not productivity busywork. Green is used exclusively for completions and success states, reinforcing the reward loop. The experience is frictionless: one tap to add a task, one tap to complete it.

---

## Competitive Positioning

Reps sits at the intersection of **habit trackers** (Streaks, Habitica, Habit — Daily Tracker) and **task managers** (Things 3, Todoist, TickTick), but with a distinct athletic performance angle.

- Unlike **Streaks** (icon/habit-centric), Reps supports full task descriptions, categories, and rich analytics
- Unlike **Things 3** (project/context-based), Reps is laser-focused on *daily recurring tasks* with *performance metrics*
- Unlike **Todoist** or **TickTick** (subscription-based, cross-platform), Reps is a one-time purchase, iOS-only, and privacy-first

The $4.99 lifetime Pro pricing is a direct counter-positioning against subscription fatigue in the productivity app market.
